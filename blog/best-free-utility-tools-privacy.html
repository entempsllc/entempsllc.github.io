The Best Free Utility Tools in 2026: 100% Privacy & No Uploads

Published: June 9, 2026 • By Entemps LLC

In 2026, the internet is flooded with "free" tools that secretly harvest your data. Whether you're combining tax documents or checking your resume against an Applicant Tracking System (ATS), uploading sensitive files to a random server is a massive privacy risk.

That's why client-side utility tools are becoming the gold standard. Client-side means the tool runs entirely in your browser—your files never leave your computer.

Here are some of the most critical, privacy-first free utility tools you can start using today.

1. Free Online Resume Scanner (ATS Checker)

When you're applying for jobs, getting past the ATS (Applicant Tracking System) is your first hurdle. Most online ATS scanners require you to create an account, upload your resume, and surrender your personal data to third-party tracking.

The Entemps Free Online Resume Scanner changes that. Built as a 100% client-side application, it compares your resume against a job description locally on your machine. It extracts keywords, calculates an ATS match score, and suggests missing terms—all without ever uploading your personal information.

2. Client-Side PDF Merger

Combining PDF files is a task everyone needs to do, from freelancers sending invoices to students submitting assignments. If you search for a "free PDF merger," you'll find hundreds of sites that require you to upload your documents to their servers. For sensitive files like bank statements or medical records, this is unacceptable.

Our Private PDF Merger works differently. It uses WebAssembly to process and merge your PDFs directly within your browser. There is no upload button—just instant, secure, local file combination.

3. Freelance Hourly Rate Calculator

Transitioning to freelance work? Calculating an hourly rate that actually covers your taxes, health insurance, and business expenses can be tricky.

Use the Freelance Rate Calculator to accurately determine your minimum hourly and project rates. Like all our tools, it runs offline and stores absolutely no data.

Why Client-Side Matters: Server-based tools cost money to run, meaning they often monetize by selling your data or restricting features behind a paywall. Client-side tools leverage your device's computing power, allowing developers like Entemps LLC to offer them completely free forever.
